Thomson Reuters Graphics web-app redesign

I helped Thomson Reuters redesign their Graphics Portal which is being used by news editors, journalists, and graphics teams around the globe. The system allows TR editors and information designers to host and share images, infographics, and interactive graphics. At the same time, any of TR Graphics clients are able to to provide readers with illustrations and statistics just by copying a link to graphics and pasting it to an article .

Main focus of the project was migrating to AWS cloud, while preserving all the functionality and user-flows within front-end libraries limitations. So client asked me to improve current user experience without changing anything globally.

I've updated overall look and feel of the portal and offered local changes that improved UX on a screen level, keeping the original user-flows in place: I combined two bars into one, highlighted the portal structure using breadcrumbs and color-coded headers, introduced tabs with actions instead of another sidebar, etc.

Additionally, I've designed a new part of the system‚Äîadministration and settings‚Äîwhich used to exist out of the UI, hardcoded into config files. The new admin panel is aligned with other parts of the system providing consistent UX for all users.

As the redesign was done at early stages of the project, I've also created a design system for front-end developers with all the colors, fonts, elements‚Äîeverything that I used when designing the solution. That way the team is able to implement my designs and build new screens if necessary without my supervision on the project.
